Step 9: Soldering Switches

Now, you'll need to solder the switches (unless you have a hot-swappable PCB).

If you haven't soldered before, this step may be rather intimidating. In that case, we recommend first practicing to solder on a may pad.

The benefit of soldering on a may pad first is that you can understand how to solder/desolder and get practice making the perfect-sized beads.

If you want to just jump straight into it, you can try soldering without any practice. We recomend you are in a well-ventilated area and have all the necessary tools on hand.

Work through each switch and solder the two metal pins to the PCB. You are looking for a solder bead size such as the ones shown below:

After all the switches have been soldered, you want o retest the PCB to make sure everything is working properly.

This can be accomplished by simply running through step #4 again and if the switches are working, you can move on to the next step.
